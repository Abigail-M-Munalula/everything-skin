Biological Mapping

1.0 Skin Classifications

Profile: Hyper-Sebaceous
Oily Skin

An over-secretion of sebum from the glands, leading to "seborrhea." While sebum protects the skin, an excess creates a film that traps debris.

  • Pore StructureDilated or "stretched" pores, particularly in the mid-face region.
  • The AdvantageNaturally high lipid content provides a stronger defense against environmental aging and fine lines.
  • Primary StressorsHumidity, aggressive surfactants, and hormonal fluctuations.
Profile: Lipid-Deficient
Dry Skin

True dry skin lacks oil (lipids), not just water. This "alipidic" state means the skin lacks the natural mortar required to hold cells together.

  • Visual MarkersInvisible pores, matte finish, and a tendency toward "crepy" texture or flaking.
  • SensoryA persistent "tight" sensation, especially post-cleansing or in temperature shifts.
  • Primary StressorsArid climates, central heating, and hot water exposure.
Profile: Heterogeneous
Combination

A dual-state profile where sebaceous gland density varies. This requires "Zone-Mapping" rather than a uniform treatment approach.

  • RegionalityHyper-activity in the T-zone (forehead/nose) contrasting with lipid deficiency on the U-zone (cheeks).
  • Seasonal FluctuationsCommonly shifts toward Oily in summer and Dry in winter months.
  • Management GoalTargeted calibration—lightweight gels for the center, rich emollients for the periphery.
Profile: Reactive / Impaired
Sensitive Skin

Not just a type, but a state of neuro-sensitivity. The nerve endings are more exposed due to a fractured moisture barrier.

  • Visual MarkersErythema (redness), broken capillaries, and blotchy texture.
  • ReactivityImmediate stinging or heat sensation when applying standard synthetic fragrances or alcohols.
  • Management GoalAnti-inflammatory protocols focusing on "Cica" and barrier-identical lipids.
Profile: Eudermic
Normal Skin

The dermatological ideal. Sebum and moisture levels are in perfect equilibrium, and the barrier is structurally sound.

  • Visual MarkersFine pores, smooth texture, and consistent radiance without excess shine.
  • Management GoalPreventative maintenance. Focus on antioxidants and consistent sun protection to maintain the baseline.
  • Note"Normal" skin can still experience dehydration or occasional hormonal congestion.
Temporal Calibration

2.0 The Daily Cycle

AM: Protect + Prep

Daytime routines focus on defending the skin against environmental pollutants and UV radiation.

  • InfusionAntioxidant (Vitamin C/E) to neutralize free radicals.
  • HydrationLightweight water-binding humectants.
  • ShieldBroad-spectrum SPF 30+ (Mandatory for all types).

PM: Repair + Renew

Nighttime is the peak phase for cellular regeneration. This is when high-potency actives work best.

  • PurifyDouble-cleansing to remove lipid-soluble pollutants.
  • CorrectTargeted treatments (Retinoids or Exfoliating Acids).
  • SealHeavy-duty barrier creams to support overnight recovery.
Formulation Data

3.0 Key Active Actives

Ceramides
Waxy lipid molecules that act as the "mortar" between skin cells, essential for barrier integrity.
BEST FOR: DRY / SENSITIVE
USE: PM
Salicylic Acid
A oil-soluble BHA that penetrates deep into pores to dissolve debris and prevent breakouts.
BEST FOR: OILY / ACNE
USE: AM or PM
Niacinamide
Vitamin B3. Regulates oil, calms redness, and visibly improves enlarged pores.
BEST FOR: ALL TYPES
USE: AM/PM
